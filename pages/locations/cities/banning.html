Banning's Water Resources

Banning sits in the San Gorgonio Pass, between Mt. San Jacinto and Mt. San Gorgonio. This unique geography creates excellent groundwater recharge from snowmelt and mountain runoff. The city was historically known for its cherry orchards, which thrived on the reliable water supply.

The Banning bench area and surrounding rural properties often depend on private wells. Water depths vary but the alluvial deposits typically produce good water at reasonable depths. We've served the Pass area for years, understanding both the advantages and challenges of drilling in this wind-swept corridor.

Why Well Maintenance Matters

Your well system works hard every day. Like any mechanical system, it needs regular attention to perform reliably and last as long as possible. Most well emergencies we see could have been prevented with basic maintenance.

Regular maintenance catches small problems before they become big ones. A $200 annual inspection can prevent a $3,000 emergency pump replacement.

Our Annual Maintenance Inspection

A complete system check that covers every component of your well system:

Pump Performance Check

  • • Measure amp draw and compare to rated specs
  • • Check flow rate and pressure output
  • • Inspect for vibration or unusual sounds
  • • Verify proper cycling and shutoff

Pressure Tank Inspection

  • • Check bladder/diaphragm condition
  • • Verify air pre-charge pressure
  • • Inspect for corrosion or leaks
  • • Test pressure switch operation

Electrical System Review

  • • Inspect control panel and connections
  • • Check for loose or corroded wiring
  • • Test safety devices and overload protection
  • • Verify proper grounding

Wellhead Inspection

  • • Check well cap seal and security
  • • Inspect casing for damage or settling
  • • Verify proper drainage away from wellhead
  • • Look for signs of contamination entry points

Water Level Measurement

  • • Static water level measurement
  • • Comparison to previous readings
  • • Assessment of aquifer condition
  • • Recommendations if levels are changing

Signs Your Well Needs Attention

Don't wait for complete failure. Call us if you notice:

⚠️ Pressure fluctuations
⚠️ Pump running more often
⚠️ Changes in water clarity
⚠️ Unusual noises or vibration
⚠️ Higher electric bills
⚠️ Air sputtering from faucets

How Much Does Well Drilling Cost in San Diego?

Published January 30, 2026 | 10 min read

If you're considering drilling a private water well in San Diego County, the first question on your mind is probably: "How much will this cost?" The honest answer is that well drilling costs in San Diego range from $15,000 to over $50,000 depending on your specific situation. Let's break down exactly what affects pricing so you can budget accurately for your project.

San Diego Well Drilling Cost Overview

San Diego County presents unique challenges for well drilling. Our diverse geology—ranging from coastal sediments to mountain granite—means costs vary dramatically by location. Here's what you can generally expect in 2026:

Typical San Diego Well Drilling Costs

  • Basic Residential Well (300-400 ft): $18,000 - $28,000
  • Standard Residential Well (400-500 ft): $25,000 - $35,000
  • Deep Residential Well (500-700 ft): $35,000 - $50,000
  • Agricultural/High-Capacity Wells: $40,000 - $75,000+

*Prices include complete turnkey installation with pump and pressure system

These ranges represent complete, turnkey installations including drilling, casing, pump equipment, electrical connections, and permits. Budget quotes that seem too good to be true often exclude critical components—always ask for a detailed breakdown.

Factors That Affect Well Drilling Cost

1. Drilling Depth Required

Depth is the single biggest factor affecting well drilling cost. Every additional foot of drilling adds to your total. San Diego County well depths vary significantly by location:

  • Coastal areas (Encinitas, Carlsbad): 150-300 feet typical
  • Valley communities (Ramona, Valley Center): 300-600 feet typical
  • East County (Alpine, Jamul): 400-600 feet typical
  • Mountain areas (Julian, Palomar): 400-700+ feet typical

Learn more about typical well depths by San Diego location to estimate what your property might require.

2. Geology and Rock Type

The type of rock and soil on your property dramatically affects drilling cost and method:

  • Alluvial/decomposed granite: Easiest to drill, lowest cost per foot ($35-$50/ft)
  • Sedimentary rock: Moderate difficulty and cost ($45-$60/ft)
  • Hard granite/metamorphic: Requires air rotary, highest cost ($60-$85/ft)

Properties in Julian or Alpine with solid granite formations will cost more per foot than valley floor properties in Ramona or Fallbrook with softer decomposed granite.

3. Drilling Method

The geology dictates which drilling method we use:

  • Mud rotary drilling: Best for softer formations, faster and less expensive
  • Air rotary drilling: Required for hard rock, more equipment and time intensive
  • Cable tool drilling: Traditional method, used for specific geological conditions

Our experienced drillers assess your site and choose the most efficient method for your specific conditions, keeping costs as low as possible while ensuring a productive well.

4. Site Accessibility

Well drilling rigs are large, heavy equipment. Easy access to your drilling site keeps costs down. Factors that can increase cost include:

  • Narrow or winding access roads
  • Steep terrain requiring special equipment
  • Distance from main roads
  • Overhead obstacles (power lines, trees)
  • Need for temporary road construction

5. Casing and Well Construction

The materials used to construct your well affect both cost and longevity:

  • Steel casing: Standard choice, durable, $15-$25/ft installed
  • PVC casing: Used in certain formations, $10-$18/ft installed
  • Casing diameter: 6" residential standard, larger for high-capacity wells

What's Included in Well Drilling Cost?

When comparing quotes, ensure you're comparing apples to apples. A complete well drilling project should include:

Drilling Phase

  • Site preparation and mobilization
  • Actual drilling to required depth
  • Steel or PVC casing installation
  • Well screen in water-bearing zones
  • Gravel pack around screen
  • Sanitary seal at surface
  • Well development (cleaning and surging)

Pump Installation

  • Submersible pump sized for your depth and demand
  • Drop pipe to pump
  • Electrical wiring and controls
  • Pressure tank (typically 40-80 gallon)
  • Pressure switch and gauge
  • Check valve and pitless adapter

Permits and Testing

  • County well permit ($200-$500)
  • Well completion report filing
  • Water quality testing (basic bacteria and minerals)
  • Flow rate testing

Financing Options for Well Drilling

We understand that well drilling is a significant investment. Here are financing options many San Diego homeowners use:

Home Equity Loans or HELOCs

If you have equity in your home, a home equity loan or line of credit often provides the lowest interest rates for well drilling projects. The well adds value to your property, making this a sound investment.

Personal Loans

Many banks and credit unions offer personal loans that can cover well drilling costs. Terms typically range from 3-7 years with competitive rates for borrowers with good credit.

USDA Rural Development Loans

Properties in qualifying rural areas of San Diego County (much of East County and North County) may be eligible for USDA loans that can include well drilling costs. These loans often feature favorable terms and low down payments.

Contractor Financing

We work with financing partners to offer payment plans for qualifying customers. Ask about our financing options when you request your estimate.

Why Well Drilling Is a Smart Investment

While the upfront cost may seem significant, a private well provides long-term value:

  • No monthly water bills: Average San Diego water bills run $80-$150/month—that's $1,000-$1,800/year saved
  • Water independence: No restrictions during droughts or rationing periods
  • Property value: A producing well adds $10,000-$30,000+ to property value
  • Agricultural use: Irrigate without worrying about tiered water rates
  • Quality water: San Diego well water is often superior to treated municipal water

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to drill a well in San Diego County?

Well drilling in San Diego County typically costs between $15,000 and $50,000 or more, depending on depth required, geology, and equipment needed. Most residential wells fall in the $20,000-$35,000 range for a complete turnkey installation including pump and pressure system.

What is the cost per foot to drill a well in San Diego?

Well drilling in San Diego typically costs $35-$85 per foot for drilling alone, depending on rock hardness and drilling method required. Softer alluvial soils cost less while hard granite formations require more expensive air rotary drilling methods.

Does well drilling cost include the pump installation?

Some quotes include pump installation while others don't—always ask. A complete turnkey installation should include drilling, casing, screen, well seal, submersible pump, pressure tank, electrical connections, and all permits. Budget an additional $3,000-$8,000 for pump equipment if not included.

Are there financing options for well drilling in San Diego?

Yes, many well drilling companies offer financing options. Some homeowners use home equity loans, personal loans, or credit lines. The USDA also offers rural development loans that can cover well drilling costs for qualifying properties.
